# obi_threshold_not_binding_at_0.32_total_imbalance_threshold_or_spread_gate_controls_000660_entry_frequency

Observation: Loosening OBI threshold 0.35→0.32 on 000660 produced identical results to OBI=0.35 — exactly 7 roundtrips, 42.86% win rate, +0.0478% return. Zero additional entries were generated by the looser OBI gate.
Why: When OBI>0.32 fires, entry is still blocked by either spread_gate_bps=18 or total_imbalance_threshold=0.20. The OBI condition is met more often at 0.32, but the other gates prevent those additional ticks from becoming trades. total_imbalance_threshold=0.20 is likely redundant with OBI>0.32 (directional OBI already implies aggregate imbalance), making it a silent duplicate filter that wipes out marginal OBI=0.32–0.35 entries.
How to apply next: Test total_imbalance_threshold=0.10 (or remove it entirely) while holding OBI=0.32 and spread_gate=18 to isolate whether it is the binding gate. If trade count increases, imbalance_threshold was the blocker; if not, the spread gate or tick-data density is the true ceiling.
